What is Timeline Space?

It's a timelining tool for plotting stories and managing events.

Think of it as being like Scrivener's corkboard without the awful, non-intuitive interface and bloated functionality, or like Microsoft Project with more free-form construction.

How does it work?

You create the scenes on your timeline, filling them with as much detail as you want. You can tag them, and connected them to other scenes in your timeline. And when you've completed your timeline, you can export it into a new Google Doc, ready to be fleshed out as a full account.

This is an example of how you might use Timeline Space as a Timeline

You can see that the coloured tags indicate where each of the characters is going. You can also see that the picture of Shelob has a symbol in the top left corner indicating that there are additional levels in the timeline to drill down into, where you might describe the fight in more detail.

You don't have to use it this way. You can plonk stuff down in any way you like, in snowflakes or grid fashion, treating it just as a giant space.

Okay, that's great, but why do I need to log in with Google?

You timelines are stored on your Google Drive account. Google Drive provides a special space for applications to stick their stuff, so you won't find your files there in the normal view, but trust me, they're there. This means that you can access them through timeline.space from any computer you log into your Google account with.

The images you put on your timelines are hosted on imgur.

They're both far, far more reliable than this little webpage, I'm sure. Nothing is hosted on timeline.space, so all your IP is out of my hands.
